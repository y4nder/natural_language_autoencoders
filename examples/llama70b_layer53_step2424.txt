NLA (Natural Language Autoencoder) — WORKED EXAMPLE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

An NLA pair = two fine-tuned LMs that together map activation vectors
to natural language and back:

  AV (activation verbalizer)  : hidden-state vector  →  text
                                [injected as a 1-token embedding into
                                 a fixed prompt, then autoregressive]

  AR (activation reconstructor): text  →  hidden-state vector
                                [truncated K+1-layer LM + Linear(d,d)
                                 head, extract at final token]

Together they form a round-trip: extract vector → AV verbalizes →
AR reconstructs → MSE(reconstructed, original) measures how well the
verbalization captured the vector's content. This MSE was the RL reward
signal during training — low MSE = the AR can recover the original
direction from the AV's words alone.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AV checkpoint:     kitft/Llama-3.3-70B-NLA-L53-av
AR checkpoint:     kitft/Llama-3.3-70B-NLA-L53-ar
Extraction model:  meta-llama/Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct
Extraction:        layer 53 residual stream (= HF hidden_states[54], output of block 53)
d_model:           8192
NLA inference:     temp=0 (greedy — reproducible)
AR mse_nrm:        MSE of L2-normalized vectors (both scaled to √d=90.51). Equals 2(1-cos) — geometrically
                   model-agnostic. Range: 0 (perfect) — 2 (orthogonal).
fve_nrm:           1 - mse_nrm / 0.9169. Denominator is Var(v_nrm) from the TRAINING dataset — the
                   predict-the-mean baseline MSE. This is the cross-model comparable metric.
