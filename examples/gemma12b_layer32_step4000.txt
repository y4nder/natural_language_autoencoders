NLA (Natural Language Autoencoder) — WORKED EXAMPLE
════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

An NLA pair = two fine-tuned LMs that together map activation vectors
to natural language and back:

  AV (activation verbalizer)     : hidden-state vector  →  text
                                   [injected as a 1-token embedding into
                                    a fixed prompt, then autoregressive]

  AR (activation reconstructor)   : text  →  hidden-state vector
                                   [truncated K+1-layer LM + Linear(d,d)
                                    head, extract at final token]

Together they form a round-trip: extract vector → AV verbalizes →
AR reconstructs → MSE(reconstructed, original) measures how well the
verbalization captured the vector's content. This MSE was the RL reward
signal during training — low MSE = the AR can recover the original
direction from the AV's words alone.

────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
AV checkpoint:     kitft/nla-gemma3-12b-L32-av
AR checkpoint:     kitft/nla-gemma3-12b-L32-ar
Extraction model:  google/gemma-3-12b-it
Extraction:        layer 32 residual stream (= HF hidden_states[33], output of block 32)
d_model:           3840
NLA inference:     temp=0 (greedy — reproducible)
Critic mse_nrm:    MSE of L2-normalized vectors (both scaled to √d=61.97). Equals 2(1-cos) — geometrically
                   model-agnostic. Range: 0 (perfect) — 2 (orthogonal).
fve_nrm:           1 - mse_nrm / 0.0302. Denominator is Var(v_nrm) from the TRAINING dataset — the
                   predict-the-mean baseline MSE. This is the cross-model comparable metric.

   ⚠ LOW-VARIANCE LAYER: this model/layer has Var(v_nrm)=0.0302 — activations are highly
   concentrated around their mean. mse_nrm/cos compress into a narrow range (everything
   looks ~0.99 cos). fve_nrm is the informative metric here: it measures improvement
   over the trivial predict-the-mean baseline, which is already very good at low variance.

  All decodes are temp=0 (greedy) — reproducible on same SGLang version

  ||v|| is the RAW L2-norm. Every vector is rescaled to
  L2=80000 (injection_scale from nla_meta.yaml)
  before injection. Raw norm is a diagnostic: outliers are OOD —
  normalization fixes magnitude but not the direction-regime.

  SYSTEM PROMPT positions (first 4) are under-sampled in
  training — datagen uses min_position=50 which for default system
  prompts (~20 tokens) skips them entirely. Incidental mechanism,
  intentional outcome: system prompts are identical across
  conversations, heavy sampling would overfit. Summary excludes
  these; user question + reply are in-distribution.

  MSE / cos columns: critic's reconstruction of the activation from
  the NLA decode text. MSE<0.5 ≈ good fidelity; MSE~2 ≈ orthogonal
  (random). cos is the same signal (MSE = 2(1-cos) under L2 norm).
